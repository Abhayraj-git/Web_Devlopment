DRAM

  • DRAM is the RAM in which data is stored in a storage cell, consisting of a transistor and a capacitor.
  • Unlike SRAM, the DRAM needs to be continuously refreshed with power supply because the capacitor has the tendency to get discharged.
  • DRAM retains the data for a very short span of time, even after the power supply is switched off.
  • Example: Main Memory
