Magnetic Memory

  • Magnetic memory, or magnetic storage, is a non-volatile technology that uses magnetism to store digital data. It encodes binary information (0s and 1s) by aligning the magnetic polarity of tiny domains on a medium, such as a metal platter or plastic tape.
  • The process involves a read/write head, which is an electromagnet. To write data, the head changes the magnetic orientation of the surface. To read data, it detects the existing magnetic fields, inducing an electrical current that is translated back into digital signals.
  • The most common examples are the Hard Disk Drive (HDD) and Magnetic Tape. HDDs offer high capacity and random access but are slower and more fragile than modern solid-state drives (SSDs). Magnetic tape is primarily used for cost-effective, long-term archival storage (sequential access).
